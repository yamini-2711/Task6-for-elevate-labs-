#  Task 6: Password Strength Evaluation

##  Objective
To understand what makes a password strong and evaluate its strength using online password checking tools.

---

## Tools Used
- Online Password Strength Checker (e.g., https://passwordmeter.com)

---

## Passwords Tested

| Password | Length | Strength Score | Strength Level |
|----------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| password123 | 11 | Low | Weak |
| HelloWorld1 | 11 | Medium | Moderate |
| Str0ng@Pass | 12 | ~70% | Strong |
| S3cur3#Access!789 | 18 | ~90% | Very Strong |
| BlueTiger$RunsFast2026! | 25 | 100% | Excellent |

---

## Analysis
- Short and simple passwords are easy to crack.
- Adding uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols improves strength.
- Longer passwords provide better security.
- Complex passwords resist common attacks more effectively.

---

 ## Common Password Attacks

### 1. Brute Force Attack
Attempts all possible combinations until the correct password is found.

### 2. Dictionary Attack
Uses a list of common words and passwords to guess credentials.

---

## Best Practices for Strong Passwords
- Use at least 12–16 characters  
- Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols  
- Avoid common words and predictable patterns  
- Do not use personal information  
- Use unique passwords for different accounts  
- Prefer passphrases (e.g., `BlueTiger$RunsFast2026!`)  

---

## Conclusion
Password complexity and length significantly improve security. Strong passwords help protect against brute force and dictionary attacks, making systems more secure.
